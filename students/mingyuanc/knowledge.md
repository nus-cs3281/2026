### Claude Code & Copilot

* **Claude Code** (CLI), **Copilot**, explored the use of AI as agents to automate tasks, plan and also be brought up to speed on certain aspect of code.
  * Used AI agents to help build with the migration of e2e test.
  * Used AI to assist in debugging. This happened when assisting the CS3281 students with their bugs. I used the error and told them everything I know and also my intuition on where to look. The AI would then suggest potential reason and help me read through more files at a faster speed. 
  * Used AI to help me plan out features. I would present the AI with my objective and constraints and also a potential plan, then the AI would break down the tasks and also highlight to me possible new constraints. I used this for the MS entra plan.
* **Skills**: I learned about **Context Management** and **Prompt engineering**, which entails writing precise instructions for AI to generate accurate code whilst managing how much context the models needs for complex task. I learnt that providing too much context could backfire and trigger ralph loops. Furthermore, I also learnt how to break down complex tasks into smaller bite sized tasks that is perfect for the AI's context.

### Copilot Code Review

* **GitHub Copilot for Code Review**:  I focused on using github copilot to augment my review process to generate PR summaries and identify logic flaws or style inconsistencies. I realized that it is really good at picking out minute details and inconsistencies across files that may or may not be in the PR. I found it most useful as an addition not a replacement of the manual review process whereby the manual reviewer should focus on business logic and correctness whereas the AI would focus on code style, best practices and linting.
